Agedashi Tofu

Agedashi tofu is a delightful Japanese dish that has gained popularity for its unique texture and flavor. It consists of cubes of soft tofu that are lightly dusted with potato starch and then deep-fried until they have a subtly crisp exterior. The tofu is then served in a hot broth made of dashi, mirin, and soy sauce, which complements the delicate taste of the tofu. Garnished with finely chopped green onions, grated daikon, and sometimes bonito flakes, it's a dish that balances simplicity with depth of flavor. The contrast between the crispy outside and the soft, almost custard-like inside of the tofu makes agedashi tofu a favorite appetizer at many Japanese restaurants. Whether enjoyed at a bustling izakaya or made at home with care, agedashi tofu is a testament to the versatility and understated elegance of tofu in Japanese cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Medium-firm tofu (momen dofu) or soft tofu (kinugoshi dofu): Start with medium-firm tofu for easier handling.
  • Potato starch: Use potato starch instead of cornstarch for coating the tofu.
  • Cooking oil: For deep-frying or shallow-frying.
  • Tsuyu sauce: A warm sauce made from dashi (or vegan-friendly kombu dashi), mirin, and soy sauce.
  • Garnishes: Grated daikon radish, chopped green onion, grated ginger, and katsuobushi (dried bonito flakes).

Steps

  1. Drain the Tofu:

  2. Make Tsuyu Sauce:
    • Combine dashi, mirin, and soy sauce to create the flavorful sauce.
    • Keep the sauce warm.
  3. Cut and Coat Tofu:
    • Cut the tofu block into 6 pieces (about 1.5-2 inch cubes).
    • Coat the tofu with potato starch.
  4. Deep Fry:
    • Deep-fry the tofu for 2 minutes until lightly browned.
    • Drain excess oil.
    • Place the tofu in a bowl.
    • Pour the warm tsuyu sauce over it.
    • Garnish with grated daikon, chopped green onion, ginger, and bonito flakes
